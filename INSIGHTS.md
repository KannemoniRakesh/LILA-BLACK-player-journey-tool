Insights – Player Journey Visualization Tool
________________________________________
📌 Overview
Using the Player Journey Visualization Tool, several behavioural patterns were identified by analysing:
•	Player movement (traffic heatmaps)
•	Combat events (kill zones)
•	Death distribution (death heatmaps)
•	Timeline progression
These insights are valuable for level design, balancing, and gameplay optimization.
________________________________________
🔥 Insight 1: Combat is concentrated along high-traffic paths
📊 Observation
•	Kill zone heatmaps strongly overlap with major movement routes
•	Players frequently engage in combat while rotating across the map
📍 Evidence
•	Traffic heatmap shows dense player movement along specific paths
•	Kill heatmap highlights the same areas as combat hotspots
🧠 Interpretation
Players are not randomly fighting — combat happens during transitions between zones, not just at destinations.
🎯 Design Implication
•	These paths act as natural chokepoints
•	Designers can:
o	Add cover or terrain variation to improve combat experience
o	Intentionally shape these paths to control engagement frequency
________________________________________
⚠️ Insight 2: Death clusters indicate potentially unfair or high-risk zones
📊 Observation
•	Death heatmaps show concentrated areas where many players die
•	Some areas show high deaths but relatively fewer kills
📍 Evidence
•	Death zones appear in locations not always aligned with kill zones
•	Indicates deaths may not always be from direct player combat
🧠 Interpretation
These could represent:
•	Poor visibility zones
•	Ambush-heavy areas
•	Environmental disadvantages (terrain, exposure)
🎯 Design Implication
•	These zones may feel unfair or frustrating
•	Designers can:
o	Improve visibility or lighting
o	Add escape routes or defensive options
o	Rebalance terrain or cover
________________________________________
🎒 Insight 3: Loot locations influence player clustering and combat
📊 Observation
•	Loot markers are often surrounded by:
o	High player traffic
o	Increased kill activity
📍 Evidence
•	Overlap between loot events, movement density, and kill zones
•	Players converge on loot-rich areas early in the match
🧠 Interpretation
Loot acts as a magnet for player interaction, driving both movement and combat.
🎯 Design Implication
•	Loot placement directly affects game pacing
•	Designers can:
o	Spread loot to reduce early congestion
o	Cluster loot to encourage early combat
o	Adjust rarity to influence player routes
________________________________________
⏱️ Insight 4: Engagement intensity increases as the match progresses
📊 Observation
•	Timeline playback shows:
o	Early game → dispersed movement
o	Mid/late game → clustered movement and combat
📍 Evidence
•	As time progresses, player paths converge
•	Kill events become more frequent in smaller areas
🧠 Interpretation
This reflects natural battle royale behavior:
•	Players are forced into smaller zones
•	Encounters become inevitable
🎯 Design Implication
•	Late-game zones are critical for player experience
•	Designers can:
o	Ensure fair terrain in final zones
o	Avoid overly open or overly restrictive areas

The data shows that:
•	Player behavior is highly structured, not random
•	Movement, loot, and combat are deeply interconnected
•	Map design directly shapes player interaction patterns
